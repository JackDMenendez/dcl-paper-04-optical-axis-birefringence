## Why v1.0

v1.0 is the first public deposit of Paper IV, and it establishes a citable
reference for the rest of the A=1 Discrete Causal Lattice series. The paper
reports a clean survivor beside a clean no-go along the lattice's optical axis
$\hat{\mathbf{n}}_* = (1,1,-1)/\sqrt3$: a birefringence cancellation that holds
for any three-hop lattice (given the geometric blocks), and a single-domain
construction that is Lorentz-violating — in **both** the induced-photon and the
bare-matter sectors — and so excluded as constructed. Foundational escape routes
(an $O_h$-restoring vacuum, a token→matter emergence map) are stated as open.

## What the paper establishes

### Kinematic sector (matter)

- **Closed-form directional dispersion anisotropy** (`\S`2): the single-tick
  spinor structure factor is flat along $\hat{\mathbf{n}}_*$ and curved across it,
  $M_\text{eff} = \tfrac43 P_\perp$; exact eigenphase dispersion and group-velocity
  split. Verified by `exp_02_closed_form_split_check` (identities $<10^{-12}$).
- **The as-constructed exclusion** (`\S`2, `subsec:as_constructed`): read as a
  literal laboratory particle, the bare single-tick spinor has an exact axial flat
  band, an $O(1)$ directional kinetic anisotropy, and an $\omega=0$ amplitude
  filter that accumulates *linearly* in the lattice spacing — excluded by many
  orders. Verified by `exp_05_matter_sector_order`.

### Gauge sector (induced electromagnetism)

- **Adjugate constitutive closure** $\boldsymbol{\mu}^{-1} =
  \gamma\operatorname{adj}(\boldsymbol{\varepsilon})$ (a theorem for any hop
  vectors; the Cauchy–Binet / compound-matrix identity), giving a doubled
  transverse Fresnel root: the vacuum **birefringence cancels**, universally over
  hop sets, *given the geometric blocks*. Verified by `exp_03_geometric_blocks`
  and `exp_03_dispersion_core` ($\max|v_+-v_-| = 1.6\times10^{-15}$).
- **The common-mode speed tension**: the shared photon dispersion carries an
  $O(1)$, dimension-four, factor-$\sim2$ speed anisotropy — excluded by
  cavity/Michelson–Morley isotropy bounds for the single trigonal domain.

### Falsifiability (`\S`4) and the shared root

- A per-channel no-go map: the birefringence null survives (a passed postdiction
  excluding the CPT-odd, dimension-five birefringent rivals); the single-domain
  photon and bare matter are excluded as constructed; the substrate massive
  dispersion (sidereal signature) awaits the emergence map.
- Both exclusions trace to the one arbitrary omission of the fourth cube diagonal
  that fixes the optical axis, and to the $O_h$ average that Papers I–II already
  rely on for isotropic Maxwell and emergent Lorentz.

## Reproducibility

From a fresh clone at the `v1.0` tag: `make paper` builds `build/Paper.pdf`
(pdfLaTeX + BibTeX, ~30 s). The headline checks run in seconds each:
`python src/experiments/exp_02_closed_form_split_check.py` (kinematic identities),
`python src/experiments/exp_05_matter_sector_order.py` (the as-constructed matter
exclusion), and `python src/experiments/exp_03_geometric_blocks.py` +
`exp_03_dispersion_core.py` (the null polarization split). See Appendices A and B.
