% abstract.tex
The A=1 discrete causal lattice of Paper~I~\cite{menendez2026geometryfirst} singles
out an optical axis $\hat{\mathbf{n}}_* = (1,1,-1)/\sqrt3$---the one cube
body-diagonal absent from its bipartite hop set---and this single geometric fact
organizes two distinct anisotropies, which we work to closed form. The first is
\emph{kinematic}: the single-tick spinor transfer operator has a structure-factor
magnitude that is flat along $\hat{\mathbf{n}}_*$ and curved across it, with
effective-mass tensor $M_\text{eff} = \tfrac43 P_\perp$, so propagation along the
axis and across it are inequivalent. This is a uniaxial \emph{directional dispersion
anisotropy}---an axial--transverse contrast between different propagation
directions---not a fixed-direction polarization split; we are careful to establish
what the transfer-operator eigenphase does and does not license, since the operator
is non-unitary off-axis. The second is a \emph{gauge}-sector effect, and it is here
that the term birefringence properly applies. Integrating the lattice's response to
a background field gives two geometric plaquette forms---an electric
$\boldsymbol{\varepsilon}$ (eigenvalues $\{1,4,4\}$, axis suppressed) and a magnetic
$\boldsymbol{\mu}^{-1}$ (eigenvalues $\{4,4,16\}$, axis enhanced), read off the
running engine---bound by the exact identity $\boldsymbol{\mu}^{-1} =
\operatorname{adj}(\boldsymbol{\varepsilon})$ (a theorem for any hop vectors). That
closure forces the photon dispersion to a doubled transverse root: the two
polarizations share one dispersion and the vacuum \emph{birefringence cancels}
(confirmed numerically to $10^{-15}$), leaving a factor-$\sim2$ common-mode speed
anisotropy. We report that residual as a genuine \emph{tension}: it is an order-unity,
dimension-four, direction-dependent speed of light, which for the single fixed hop-set
(a single trigonal domain) is a marginal Lorentz violation excluded by isotropy
experiments. Its origin is the same omission of the fourth cube diagonal that creates
the optical axis---the four-diagonal hop set is exactly isotropic---so the natural
resolution is structural (a more symmetric hop topology, which the formalism admits) and
is left as a foundational open question. This tension does not bear on the birefringence
null or the kinematic result. Every claim's scope, evidence, and status is recorded in
the audit table (Table~\ref{tab:audit}).
