\label{sec:code_and_data}

Every numerical claim in this paper is reproducible from the companion
repository (linked at the release DOI). The layout is:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{src/experiments/} --- the verification experiments, each a
    self-contained script with a \texttt{PASS}/\texttt{FAIL} summary:
    \begin{itemize}
      \item \texttt{exp\_01\_optical\_axis\_isotropy} --- the optical axis is
        $(1,1,-1)$ and the anisotropy survives A=1 renormalization in observable
        amplitudes.
      \item \texttt{exp\_02\_closed\_form\_split\_check} --- the closed-form
        kinematic identities (structure factor, eigenphase dispersion, group
        velocity) to $<10^{-12}$; speed coefficients to $<10^{-5}$.
      \item \texttt{exp\_05\_matter\_sector\_order} --- the as-constructed
        matter-sector consequences of Sec.~\ref{subsec:as_constructed}: exact
        flat band, $O(1)$ kinetic anisotropy, kinetic-scale ratio
        $\le 8/(9\sqrt3)$, and the linearly-accumulating $\omega=0$ filter.
      \item \texttt{exp\_03\_geometric\_blocks} --- the two gauge blocks read
        from the engine and the adjugate relation $\boldsymbol{\mu}^{-1} =
        \operatorname{adj}(\boldsymbol{\varepsilon})$ to $5\times10^{-15}$.
      \item \texttt{exp\_03\_dispersion\_core} --- the Fresnel solver on the
        engine blocks: $\max|v_+-v_-| = 1.6\times10^{-15}$ over $2000$
        directions (detuned control $0.5$).
      \item \texttt{exp\_04\_electric\_permittivity\_extraction} --- the electric
        block read off \texttt{HopOperator.step} (distinct from \texttt{dcl-core}'s
        magnetic-anchor \texttt{exp\_04}).
    \end{itemize}
  \item \texttt{src/utilities/electric\_induced\_action.py} --- the symbolic
    derivation of both blocks, the adjugate theorem for general vectors, and the
    dispersion factorization; the paper's displayed equations in
    \texttt{paper/sections/generated/} are emitted from these verified
    expressions and are never hand-transcribed.
  \item \texttt{notes/} --- the derivation and analysis notes backing each audit
    row; in particular \texttt{gauge\_common\_mode\_order.md}
    (Appendix~\ref{sec:band_sum}) and \texttt{oh\_average\_inherited\_from\_paper02.md}.
  \item \texttt{data/} --- the recorded outputs of the experiment runs.
\end{itemize}

The audit table (Table~\ref{tab:audit}) names, per row, the specific script or
note that backs it; a reader can audit any claim from that pointer plus this
appendix.
