The optical axis $\hat{\mathbf{n}}_* = (1,1,-1)/\sqrt3$, fixed geometrically as the
cube body-diagonal the hop set omits, organizes two anisotropies. In the matter
sector it produces a closed-form uniaxial \emph{directional dispersion anisotropy},
with $M_\text{eff} = \tfrac43 P_\perp$ and a massive-sector group speed
$\tfrac23\sin\omega\,|\mathbf{k}|\sin\alpha$; the A=1 normalization earns the
group-velocity reading for $\omega\neq0$, and shows the massless eigenphase to be
exactly flat (Prop.~\ref{prop:massless_flat}). In the gauge sector, the two geometric
plaquette forms obey the adjugate relation $\boldsymbol{\mu}^{-1} =
\gamma\operatorname{adj}(\boldsymbol{\varepsilon})$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:adjugate}),
which forces a doubled transverse root (Theorem~\ref{thm:doubled_root}): the vacuum
birefringence cancels.

On the paper's motivating question---what falsifies the framework along this
axis---the account is deliberately structured (Sec.~\ref{sec:falsifiability}). The
birefringence null is itself a falsifiable prediction the data have already
\emph{passed}: the framework forbids optical-axis vacuum birefringence, that quantity
is tightly bounded and unobserved, and the same non-observation excludes the CPT-odd,
birefringent Lorentz-violating rivals while corroborating this framework's
non-birefringent class. What the null does going forward is redirect the testable
content to \emph{dispersion}, which the $\omega=0$ result splits by field: a
massive-sector directional dispersion that is real but Planck-suppressed, whose
distinctive handle is a fixed-axis \emph{sidereal} signature rather than its
magnitude; a real $\omega=0$ magnitude/filtering anisotropy; and---contingently---a
\emph{photon} dispersion carried not by the massless spinor mode (which does not
disperse) but by the induced gauge field's common-mode speed anisotropy. That last
channel is gated on two derivations we leave open: the effective-action
legitimization of the constitutive blocks, and the $O_h$-restoration computation,
which may null it. Those, and a genuine effective-action derivation of the gauge
blocks, are the natural next steps, and where the framework's forward falsifiability
will be decided. The affirmed algebra---the adjugate theorem and its doubled-root
closure---stands independently of how they resolve.
