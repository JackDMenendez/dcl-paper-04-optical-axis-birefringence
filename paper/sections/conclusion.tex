The optical axis $\hat{\mathbf{n}}_* = (1,1,-1)/\sqrt3$, fixed geometrically as the
cube body-diagonal the hop set omits, organizes two anisotropies. In the matter
sector it produces a closed-form uniaxial \emph{directional dispersion anisotropy},
with $M_\text{eff} = \tfrac43 P_\perp$ and a massive-sector group speed
$\tfrac23\sin\omega\,|\mathbf{k}|\sin\alpha$; the A=1 normalization earns the
group-velocity reading for $\omega\neq0$, and shows the massless eigenphase to be
exactly flat (Prop.~\ref{prop:massless_flat}). In the gauge sector, the two geometric
plaquette forms obey the adjugate relation $\boldsymbol{\mu}^{-1} =
\gamma\operatorname{adj}(\boldsymbol{\varepsilon})$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:adjugate}),
which forces a doubled transverse root (Theorem~\ref{thm:doubled_root}): the vacuum
birefringence cancels.

On the paper's motivating question---what falsifies the framework along this
axis---the account is deliberately structured (Sec.~\ref{sec:falsifiability}). The
birefringence null is itself a stringent existing constraint the framework has already
\emph{survived}---a falsification test (an observed split would kill it) passed as a
non-trivial postdiction: the framework forbids optical-axis vacuum birefringence, that
quantity is tightly bounded and unobserved, and the same non-observation excludes the
CPT-odd, birefringent Lorentz-violating rivals while corroborating this framework's
non-birefringent class---though a further non-detection no longer \emph{discriminates}
this framework from standard electrodynamics, so the forward frontier is dispersion,
not birefringence. That content the $\omega=0$ result splits by field: a
massive-sector directional dispersion that is real but Planck-suppressed, whose
distinctive handle is a fixed-axis \emph{sidereal} signature rather than its
magnitude; a real $\omega=0$ magnitude/filtering anisotropy; and the \emph{photon}
speed itself, carried not by the massless spinor mode (which does not disperse) but by
the induced gauge field's common-mode anisotropy.

That last channel we report as a genuine \emph{tension}. The induced photon speed
anisotropy is $O(1)$ and dimension-four---not suppressed, by three independent
computations---so for the physical vacuum taken as the single fixed trigonal domain of
the A=1 hop set it is a marginal Lorentz violation excluded by isotropy experiments:
decisively for the geometric gauge completion, and strong-provisionally for the dynamical
model, pending the full effective-action Hessian (a nonzero dimension-four traceless part,
of any order-unity size, would settle it). Its
origin is sharp: the anisotropy is the single tensor $\sum_a\mathbf{V}_a\mathbf{V}_a^{\!\top}$,
which is $\{1,4,4\}$ only because the hop set omits the fourth cube diagonal $(1,1,-1)$;
the four-diagonal set is exactly isotropic. The optical axis this paper studies and this
gauge-sector tension are thus the same fact seen twice, and the natural resolution is
structural---restoring the cubic group by including the omitted axis, or by a vacuum that
superposes the four diamond orientations so the induced gauge loop sees the isotropic
average while matter retains its dimension-six axis. Whether such a construction preserves
the A=1 and Dirac structure is a foundational question for future work; we state the
tension plainly rather than resolve it here. It does not touch the birefringence null, the
adjugate theorem, or the kinematic dispersion, which stand independently.
